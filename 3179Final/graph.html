Global Warming
Global warming is the ongoing rise in the Earth's temperature caused by the accumulation of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane etc. in the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, leading to a gradual increase in global temperatures. This phenomenon is a critical driver of climate change, resulting in more frequent and severe weather events, rising sea levels, melting caps, and many more. It is primarily driven by human activities, including the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, and poses a significant threat to the environment and human well-being.

The melting of Arctic sea ice, a consequence of global warming, represents the ongoing reduction in the extent and thickness of ice in the Arctic region. Rising temperatures, primarily caused by the buildup of greenhouse gases, are causing Arctic ice to shrink, leading to profound environmental and climatic changes.
Melting of the sea ice will cause far-reaching effects including rising sea levels, animals losing their habitats, altered weather patterns, and the disruption of ecosystems. The melting of Arctic sea ice serves as a stark indicator of the planet's changing climate and underscores the need for urgent action to address the causes of global warming.

Renewable energy is energy that is collected from renewable resources, including sunlight, wind, water tides, and geothermal heat. Renewable energy often provides energy in four important areas: electricity generation, air and water heating/cooling, transportation, and rural (off-grid) energy services.

Electric cars, as a cleaner alternative to conventional motor vehicles, play a vital role in mitigating climate change. They are powered by electricity, which can be generated from renewable sources, reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Unlike traditional cars that burn fossil fuels, electric cars produce zero tailpipe emissions. This transition to electric vehicles helps combat climate change by curbing air pollution and reducing carbon emissions, contributing to a more sustainable and environmentally friendly transportation future.
